3D Crowd Simulation

Description

This project focused on simulating crowd movement within real 3D urban environments by integrating multiple crowd simulation tools with public cadastral data and accurate road maps sourced from government websites. By querying the locations and forms of buildings and incorporating detailed infrastructure layouts, the simulation provided realistic visualisations of crowd dynamics in actual cityscapes.

Area Selection

Using geographic coordinates, public datasets are queried to retrieve precise locations and metadata for buildings and roads. The simulation environment is then reconstructed using Delaunay triangulation, which partitions the urban landscape into smaller, manageable sections.

Walkable Surfaces

Walkable surfaces are identified using metadata, distinguishing accessible areas like roads and footpaths from non-walkable zones such as water, grass, or restricted regions. This information is passed to the simulation engine to guide agent movement and ensure realistic navigation.

Satellite data

Satellite data is also used to enhance understanding of the environment and to validate the accuracy of spatial features within the simulation.
